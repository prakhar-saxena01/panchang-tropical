About This Project

Why Tropical Panchang?
The Problem

There are thousands of Panchang apps — and almost all of them use Sidereal (Nirayana) zodiac with Lahiri ayanamsa. The sidereal space is completely saturated. When users request Tropical (Sayana) versions, developers often ignore them.

This app takes a different approach: Tropical Panchang — all positions computed directly from the Sun and Moon's apparent ecliptic longitudes in the tropical zodiac, with no ayanamsa correction applied.

How the Five Limbs are Computed
Tithi (lunar day)
Moon's tropical longitude minus Sun's tropical longitude, divided into 30 arcs of 12° each. Each arc = one Tithi. 1–15 = Shukla Paksha, 16–30 = Krishna Paksha.
Nakshatra (lunar mansion)
Moon's tropical longitude divided into 27 equal arcs of 13°20' each. Each arc = one Nakshatra. 4 Padas per Nakshatra (3°20' each).
Yoga
Sum of Sun's and Moon's tropical longitudes, divided into 27 equal arcs of 13°20'. The resulting arc determines the Yoga.
Karana (half-tithi)
Each Tithi is divided into two halves (6° arcs). There are 11 Karanas — 4 fixed and 7 movable repeating through the month.
Vaar (weekday)
The day of the week, each ruled by a planet in the traditional Chaldean order.
Tropical vs Sidereal

The tropical zodiac measures celestial longitude from the vernal equinox (0° Aries = March equinox). The sidereal zodiac uses a fixed star reference and is currently about 23–24° behind the tropical. Most Indian Panchang software uses sidereal. This app uses tropical — making it the rare option in the Panchang space.

Accuracy & Sources

Solar positions use a simplified VSOP87 formula (accurate to ~1 arcminute for daily use). Lunar positions use a simplified ELP2000 series (accurate to ~15–20 arcminutes). Sunrise/Sunset uses the standard astronomical algorithm. All calculations run client-side in JavaScript — no server, no data collection.
